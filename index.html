FAQ

Common questions.

Under NFPA 25, most commercial wet pipe sprinkler systems require quarterly, annual, and five-year inspections. The quarterly tests focus on gauges and alarm devices. Annual tests include main drain and trip tests. Five-year tests cover internal inspections and obstruction investigations. Your AHJ (local fire marshal) may have additional local requirements on top of the NFPA minimums.
NFPA 25 is the National Fire Protection Association's standard for the inspection, testing, and maintenance of water-based fire protection systems. It sets the minimum required frequencies and methods for inspecting sprinklers, valves, fire pumps, and more. Arizona fire marshals and insurance carriers use it as the baseline for compliance. Failing to follow NFPA 25 can void your insurance coverage and expose building owners to liability.
